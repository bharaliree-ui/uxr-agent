Usability Interview — Participant P2
Product: PlateUp (meal-planning + grocery app)
Context: Has used the app for two weeks. Reviewing the grocery list and subscription.

Moderator: How has the first couple of weeks been?
P2: Mostly good. But I don't trust the quantities on the grocery list. It told me to buy 800 grams of chicken for two people and that felt like way too much. I ended up overriding it almost every time.

Moderator: What did you do when the amount looked off?
P2: I just edited it down manually. But it made me wonder if the whole list was padded, so I started double-checking everything, which kind of defeats the point.

Moderator: Let's talk about changing recipes. Did you ever swap one out?
P2: I tried. I could not find where to do it. I looked in the recipe screen, in settings, everywhere. A friend told me you have to long-press. Who would know that?

Moderator: You mentioned the subscription earlier.
P2: Right — I genuinely don't understand what I'm paying for. There's a free tier and a "Plus" tier and the screen comparing them is confusing. I think I'm on Plus but I'm not even sure what I unlocked.

Moderator: Would you recommend it to a friend?
P2: Maybe, with an asterisk. The idea is great and it does save time. But the trust thing — if I have to check its numbers, I'm not saving as much as I hoped.
