Usability Interview — Participant P1
Product: PlateUp (meal-planning + grocery app)
Context: First-time user, walked through onboarding and planning a week of meals.

Moderator: Tell me what you expected when you first opened the app.
P1: I thought I'd just pick a few recipes and get a grocery list. But right away it asked me a ton of questions — diet, allergies, how many people, my budget, my goals. I almost quit. It felt like homework before I'd even seen anything.

Moderator: What did you do?
P1: I rushed through it and probably gave it junk answers just to get past it. So now I'm not sure the plan it made for me is even based on real preferences.

Moderator: Once you got into planning, how did it go?
P1: The meal plan itself looked nice. I liked that it told me roughly how much time each recipe takes — that saved me, because I have a toddler and I don't have an hour to cook.

Moderator: Was there anything you wanted to change?
P1: Yeah, one recipe had shrimp and I wanted to swap it. I spent a while looking for a swap button. I eventually long-pressed the recipe and a menu popped up, but I found that by accident. I'd never have guessed.

Moderator: Anything about the grocery list?
P1: It was fine. The time-saving is the main reason I'd keep using it honestly. Not the fancy stuff, just — it gave me back an evening.
