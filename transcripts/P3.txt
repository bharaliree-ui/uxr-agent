Usability Interview — Participant P3
Product: PlateUp (meal-planning + grocery app)
Context: New user, tech-comfortable, completed onboarding and built one meal plan.

Moderator: Walk me through your first few minutes.
P3: The signup-slash-onboarding was long. So many questions before any payoff. I get why — it wants to personalize — but it should let me skip and fill that in later. I'd rather see the product first and earn my trust before I hand over all that.

Moderator: Did you finish the onboarding?
P3: I did, but reluctantly. If I weren't doing this session I might have bailed around the budget question.

Moderator: How did the planning feel?
P3: Good once I was in. Clean. I appreciated the cook-time estimates — that's the kind of detail that actually changes what I'd pick on a weeknight.

Moderator: Did you try editing the plan at all?
P3: I wanted to replace the salmon dish. I tapped around the recipe card for a while. There was no visible edit or swap control. I finally found it through a long-press, which is not discoverable at all. On a first run I'd assume the feature just didn't exist.

Moderator: Last thing — overall impression?
P3: Promising. The core value is real, it saves planning effort. The rough edges are all about getting started and finding controls, not the cooking part.
