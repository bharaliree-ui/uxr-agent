Usability Interview — Participant P4
Product: PlateUp (meal-planning + grocery app)
Context: Three-week user, cooks for a family of four. Discussing ongoing use and value.

Moderator: What keeps you coming back?
P4: Time, honestly. Before this I'd stand in the kitchen at 6pm with no plan. Now the week is decided and the shopping is mostly done for me. That's worth a lot.

Moderator: Anything that frustrates you?
P4: The portion amounts. For a family of four it sometimes suggests amounts that seem random — too much one week, too little the next. I've learned to sanity-check the meat and produce before I order.

Moderator: Does that affect your trust in it?
P4: A little. I trust the recipes, but not the math. I wish it showed me how it calculated a quantity so I could believe it.

Moderator: Have you explored the paid features?
P4: I upgraded but I couldn't tell you exactly what changed. The billing screen and the feature list don't line up in my head. I pay it and hope I'm getting the good version.

Moderator: If the team could fix one thing, what should it be?
P4: Make me trust the numbers. The time-saving already won me over — but the second-guessing on quantities is the thing that nags at me every single order.
